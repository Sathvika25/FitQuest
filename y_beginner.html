Welcome to Beginner level Yoga!!


Bhramari Pranayama

The Bhramari Pranayama lowers one's blood pressure, thus relieving hypertension. It releases cerebral tension, hence it is recommended as a nightly routine yoga for better sleep.

This asan is recommended to be performed 2 - 5 times a day for better results.

Nadi shodhana

Nadi shodhana is a pranayama practice where the breath is consciously, physically regulated to enhance the flow of air through the nostrils as a method to balance the energy of the body and the mind.

Practice five to ten rounds every day for at least one month.

Sitali Pranayama

Sitali is a kind of breathing technique that cools the body, adds moisture to the system and soothes a Pitta imbalance.

You can start with three minutes of Sitali breath and gradually aim to build up to a 10-minute practice every day.

Bhujangasana

Bhujangasana or Cobra Pose is a reclining back-bending asana in hatha yoga and modern yoga as exercise.

Initially, practice the Asana three times holding each repetition briefly.

Tadasana

Tadasana is a basic posture in Yoga and part of many other series of asanas (Example: Suryanamaskar). It has several benefits, especially for toning the abdominal muscles and strengthening the lower body.

As a beginner, you can do this Tadasana procedure for ten to twelve seconds about two to three times a day.

Uttanpaadasana

Both your feet and knees should be kept together and your breathing should be normal. Exhale and while breathing in, slowly raise the legs to 90 degree angle from the door without jerk. Do not raise the shoulders from the ground. Breathe normally in the final position and hold for few seconds.

You may practise raised leg pose for five seconds, and if comfortable, you may increase the holding time to 30 seconds.
