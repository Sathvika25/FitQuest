Welcome to Intermediate level Yoga!!


Ardha Chakrasana

Ardha Chakrasana is a yoga pose that combines deep backbending with an intense upper body stretch. It resembles a half wheel, hence which explains the name of the yoga pose. It is an intermediate-level asana that requires flexibility.

This asan is recommended to be performed 3 - 5 times a day for better flexibility.

Ardha Halasana

Ardha Halasana is an intermediate-level asana that might improve physiological variables like resting heart rate, diastolic blood pressure, and systolic blood pressure.

Practice anywhere from 30 seconds to a few minutes every day.

Salabhasana

Salabhasana or Locust Pose effectively preps practicers for deeper backbends, strengthening the back of the torso, legs, and arms. It improves posture and counteracts the effects of prolonged sitting. It may help relieve lower back pain

You can repeat this asana 1-3 times. It would be best if you concentrated on the back and leg muscles

Trikonasana

rikonasana is a posture of the Hatha Yoga style of Yoga.It helps in stretching the legs and strengthening body muscles. It activates the body core, which further stimulates the digestive organs.

During Trikonasana yoga, maintain the pose for a duration of 10 to 30 seconds on each side, alternating sides between 3 to 6 repetitions .

Uttana Mandukasana

Uttana mandukasana can provide several benefits beyond the mat; this asana may help in managing cervical spondylosis, asthma, text neck syndrome, stress, arthritis and help improve cognition

As a intermediate-level practicer you may repeat this asana six times if you feel comfortable.

Vrikshasana

Vrikshasana calms and relaxes the central nervous system and stretches the entire body. It strengthens the legs and opens the hips and improves your neuromuscular coordination and endurance.

One can stay in Vrikshasana approximately for 1 minute on each leg
